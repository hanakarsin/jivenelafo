Kenya census 2019 results breakdown: Everything you need to know

February 27, 2024
Post

Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) is the principal agency mandated by the government to collect, analyze and disseminate statistical data in the country. KNBS carries out these roles by overseeing the whole census exercise carried out every ten years. Census allows the national government to re-align policies and programs that pertain to development. In August 2019, the government, through KNBS carried out 2019 census. After a fruitful exercise, KNBS director-general has released Kenya census 2019 results.

Kenya National Bureau of Statistics has released the results of Kenya census 2019 indicating the population of the country. KNBS director-general, Mr. Zachary Mwangi publicly released the results on Monday 4th November 2019. The exercise was conducted on 24th-31st August 2019. The national government, through the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, hired thousands of citizens to carry out the activity in various capacities such as ICT supervisors, content supervisors, and enumerators. In the results, the country’s population stands at 47.6 million individuals.

Kenya population ratio

Kenya census 2019 results also provide the male to female ratio, indicating that the country has more females than males. The total number of females in the country is 24, 101,716 as compared to the male individuals who make a total of 23, 548, 056.

Intersex population

Kenya National Bureau of Statistics has for the very first time in history recorded the number of intersex individuals in the country. Kenya census 2019 results show that Nairobi County has the highest number of intersex individuals followed by one of its neighbouring counties, Kiambu. The total number of intersex individuals in the entire country is 1, 564.

Kenya population and housing census

One of the primary aims of the 2019 census was to establish the household sizes of the country per county.

The county with the leading household size is Mandera. The average household size of this county is 6.9 individuals per family.

Mandera is closely followed by Wajir with 6.1 persons per family. Garissa comes in the 3rd place with an average of 5.9 persons, 5.8 individuals in Marsabit and 5.6 persons in Turkana.

West Pokot County in Rift Valley was also recorded among the counties with high household sizes of more than five persons. The county has 5.3 individuals per family, while Kwale County has 5.0 persons in every family.

There is an average of 3.0 persons per family in Nyeri, Central, Kiambu, and Kirinyaga. For Embu and Muranga, the average household size per family stands at 3.3 persons.

A general overview of the results

From the results of the 2019 census, the country’s population has experienced an overall increment of close to 10 million individuals. In 2009, the population was 37.7 million as compared to the current 47.6 million people.

The intersex population has had a significant decline from 2.9% in 2009 to 2.2 % in 2019. Currently, Kenyans have fewer children, and this causes a decrement from 4.2 in 2009 to 3.9 in 2019.

Just like the 2009 reports, Kenya census 2019 population by tribes indicates that the tribe with the highest population is the Kikuyu followed by the Luhya, Luo, Kalenjin, Kamba, Kisii, Meru and lastly other African tribes. However, actual percentages and figures in regards to the above population per age group are yet to be publicized.
