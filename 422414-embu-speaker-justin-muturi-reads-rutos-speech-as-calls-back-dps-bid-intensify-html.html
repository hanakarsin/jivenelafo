Embu: Speaker Justin Muturi Reads Ruto's Speech As Calls to Back DP's Bid Intensify

August 10, 2024
Post
  • Two Embu brothers who died at the hands of police officers were laid to rest at their father’s farm in Kithangari Kianjokoma in Embu North on Friday, August 13
  • The burial service was attended by hundreds of mourners and a few politicians
  • During the emotional send-off, National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi read DP William Ruto's speech raising speculations

National Assembly Speaker Justin Muturi, on Friday, August 13, read a condolence message to the family of the late Kianjokoma brothers on behalf of Deputy President William Ruto.

This raised speculation on whether he plans to join the so-called hustler nation associated with the DP.

Part of Ruto's message read:

"As a nation, we are repulsed by this tragedy and we demand that the Kenya Police Service officers treat fellows citizens dignity and humanely."

In his own speech, Muturi joined other leaders in demanding justice for the duo.

''I once again expressed my deepest sympathies to the family following this tragic loss. As we continue to demand justice for the slain young people, I call upon all the security organs concerned to expeditiously conclude their investigations and bring all those culpable to book,'' said Muturi.

Muturi was coronated as the Mt Kenya spokesperson on Saturday, May 22, an event Ruto welcomed.

Emotional send-off

The burial service for the late Embu brothers Benson Njiru and Emmanuel Muturi, who died after being arrested by police for allegedly breaking curfew rules, was held at Kianjokoma Primary School.

The emotional send off was attended by hundreds of mourners and politicians from across the country.

The two were later laid to rest at their father’s farm in Kithangari Kianjokoma in Embu North.

Some mourners donned t-shirts with the message 'Justice for Benson and Emmanuel'.

Autopsy reveals duo died of multiple head and rib injuries

An autopsy conducted on the bodies of the two brothers revealed they died of multiple head and rib injuries.

The report showed 23-year-old Benson Njiru and his younger brother Emmanuel Mutura, 19, died from the injuries inflicted by a blunt object.

This was according to post-mortem results released at the Embu Level Five Hospital mortuary. The autopsy was conducted by pathologist Kamau Wangari.

The physician stated that Mutura's head was deformed and had bruises on the face, while Njiru suffered broken ribs and brain injuries.
