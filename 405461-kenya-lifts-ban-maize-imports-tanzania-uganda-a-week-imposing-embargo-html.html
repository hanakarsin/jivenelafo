Kenya lifts ban on maize imports from Tanzania, Uganda a week after imposing embargo

May 03, 2024
Post

- Kenya banned the importation of maize from the two regional nations Friday, March 5, over an increase in chronic aflatoxin-related illnesses

- Business people exporting maize into Kenya from the two countries will be required to have a certificate of origin from the counties of produce among other requirements

- Ministry of Agriculture says that the move is aimed at ensuring the safety of consumers

It is a sigh of relief for Tanzania and Uganda after the Kenyan government moved to lift a ban on maize imports from the two neighboring countries.

Kenya had banned the importation of maize from the two nations indicating that their crops exhibited an acute increase in chronic aflatoxin-related illnesses, some of which had resulted in death.

But in a sudden turn of events, Kenya’s Ministry of Agriculture said that all stakeholders dealing in maize imports would be required to be registered.

All the consignments coming into Kenya must be accompanied by a certificate of conformity on aflatoxin levels and that traders have to issue details of their warehouses where the product will be stored upon entering the country.

The certificate of conformity should indicate that the aflatoxin levels comply with the maximum required levels of 10 parts per billion.

Traders importing maize from Uganda to Kenya will be required to also be in possession of a certificate of origin from the counties of produce before they get clearance at the respective border points.

Agriculture Chief Administrative Secretary Lawrence Angolo said in a statement that the move was aimed at addressing the safety of consumers and that the country will not be compromised on the same.

Angolo said the details on the warehouse would help in ensuring the maize supplied to Kenya conformed to all standard procedures on food handling and that it was not dried traditionally on roads.

“While we strive to give Kenya safe food by addressing the challenge in the production system, we equally expect our trading partners to trade safe maize as per the East African community standards,” said Angolo.

The ministry further petitioned the two regional countries to fast track ratification of East Africa Community (EAC- SGS) standards on aflatoxin and submit the instruments of certification to the EAC.

Uganda exports at least 90% of its maize to Kenya with an increasing average of 330,620 tonnes.

According to data from the Ministry of Agriculture, in the past year, Uganda’s maize exports stood at 351,420 tonnes with a monthly average of 50,203 tonnes.
