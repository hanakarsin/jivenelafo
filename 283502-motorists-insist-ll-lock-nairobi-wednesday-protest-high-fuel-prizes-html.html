Motorists insist theyll lock down Nairobi on Wednesday to protest high fuel prizes

July 22, 2024
Post

- Motorists will park their vehicles on major highways in protest

- Commuters have been urged to mind their safety and travel before 7.30am

- Motorists Association of Kenya says the protests will continue if nothing will be done after Wednesday's protest

Motorists in Nairobi have vowed to paralyse transport in Nairobi on Wednesday, August 22 in a mega protest even as human rights activists take to the streets to lobby for the release of Uganda celebrity MP Bobi Wine.

TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported that the Motorists Association of Kenya (MAK) had through their Chairman Peter Murima announced to stage the protest against a government move to have 16% VAT on petroleum products.

According to MAK boss, the protest where motorist will be required to pack their vehicles on all major highways, will kick off from 7.30am to 10.30am.

An advice note from Insight, which was seen by TUKO.co.ke, has advised commuters to anticipate paralysis in transportation and at the same time called on the citizenry to be cautious of their safety.

The protests are also expected in other major towns with MAK threatening to stage another one on Wednesday, August 29 if the government does not shelve its taxation move.

Various social media users have lauded the planned process expressing optimism that they will go along way in expressing their grievances to the government which they claim has turned a deaf ear on the serious issue.

On the contrary, others have raised concerns that the protests are a security threat besides also posing as a great inconvenience for those headed at work and those who had planned to travel.

According to Murima, the planned taxes will see taxpayers cough at least KSh 70 for every liter of petrol as taxes, claiming it is the highest on earth.

The Kenya Private Sector Alliance (KEPSA) had also warned on Monday, August, 20 the new tax will raise the country's inflation by another 4% and also stifle the attainment of the Big Four agenda.
