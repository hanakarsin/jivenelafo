Renowned Genge rapper Mejja is infuriated after his latest hit Tabia Za Wakenya was pulled down from YouTube following a copyright strike.

The song had amassed over 1.2 million views and was pulled down from YouTube after an upcoming artist complained.

In a statement released on June 11 via Instagram, Mejja said Tabia Za Wakenya bears no similarities with the song he allegedly sampled.

Mejja said he was speechless when he received an email from YouTube informing him about the copyright strike.

He swore to deal with the matter, saying that the move to pull down Tabia Za Wakenya was informed by hate.

Mejja also decried YouTube’s rule stipulating that a video pulled down due to a copyright strike is re-uploaded after two weeks.

An upcoming musician named Revina claimed he had recorded the song with music producer Vicky Pondis and the producer allegedly colluded with Mejja to steal the piece.

Mejja termed the allegations hateful, adding that naysayers would not dissuade him.

“Hate on another level, I am the same person helping new artists. If this is meant to slow me down, I will not stop releasing music till I die,” he wrote.

Naitwa Mejja

Tabia Za Wakenya is not the rapper's first song to be pulled down from YouTube.

On May 1, 2021, YouTube pulled down Mejja's hit dubbed Naitwa Mejja featuring Nasha Travis hours after uploading it.

Mejja, however, admitted that he had sampled a beat from another artiste.
