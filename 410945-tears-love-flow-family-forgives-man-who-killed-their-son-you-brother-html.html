There was a moment of joy and regret in Taita Taveta after a man who has been serving a jail sentence for the murder of a 30-year-old man was freed.

Albert Nyati killed Haron Lenjo in 2017 and was sentenced to 30 years in prison but in 2019, he appealed his sentenced and his jail term was reduced to only five years. And with good behaviour, he was freed three and a half years after he was incarcerated.

According to a report by Daily Nation, the ex-convict first stop after he tasted freedom was at a chief's office where he met Lenjo's family and he begged them to forgive him as he sought to turn a new page.

An emotional moment unfolded as Jonathan Mwaningo, the deceased's father embraced Nyati and said he had unconditionally forgiven him.

"I forgave you a long time ago. I have no problem with you, my son," Mwaningo told a remorseful Nyati."You are our brother and friend. We forgive you and welcome you back with all blessings," added Lenjo’s brother, Wilfred Mrombo.

On his part, the ex-convict said the forgiveness was liberating and also said he regretted committing the murder, adding that if he could go back in time, he would walk away after the heated exchange he had with Lenjo.

"I wish I had walked away. I even don't want to remember what transpired. My advice to people is that they should not be carried away by anger," he said.

On the fateful day in 2017 that Lenjo died, Nyati shoved the boda boda rider during a brawl and he fell to the ground where he sustained fatal injuries.

By the time he was arriving in the hospital, he had already passed on.

Nyati said he is ready to start a new life and use the skills he gained in prison to impact the community positively.
