Kenya Films Classification Board CEO Ezekiel Mutua has attempted to teach Kenyans the right pronunciation of the next vice-president of America.

With the first black US president having such ingrained roots in Kenya, many locals wanted to also associate the first-ever black and female US vice-president elect with our homeland, going as far as calling her Khamala Harris.

As you might know, the 'Kha' would very well fit with our Kenyan brothers from the western part of the country but that is definitely a borrowed dream.

The hard-hitting moral police decided to help Kenyans pronounce the name Kamala Harris noting some might "have it rough".

Going on Facebook on Sunday, November 8, Mutua ensured he went by through each syllable to make it as easy as possible for all to understand.

He wrote:

"Let's practice. Ka.Ma... PAUSE. It gets tricky from here on! You'll hear a voice saying RA! Don't listen to that voice suggesting intestines. Think of a small packet of Maziwa Mala - Kamala. Easy, isn't it?"Now, let's try. Ka.Ma.La. Perfect!"

For the second name, Mutua used a hilarious example of the famous word 'auntie wa Harrier'.

He wrote:

"The next one is not so hard. Think of 'Auntie wa Harrier', now say Ha.RR.i.s - Harris. You might be tempted to say HaLLis, but remember your Aunt's car - HaRRis. Good: Kamala Harris, the first female and first woman of colour vice president of the USA."
