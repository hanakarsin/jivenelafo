Government dismisses claims Kenyans were forced to register for Huduma Namba

Post

- Interior PS Kibicho defended government saying registration exercise was voluntary for all

- The government official also denied claims there was discrimination against any community during registration

- The PS was appearing in court as a witness in a case challenging the constitutionality of Huduma Namba

- The petition was filed by the Nubian Rights Forum and Kenya Human Rights Commission

Interior Principal Secretary (PS) Karanja Kibicho has rubbished claims Kenyans were intimidated and forced to register for the National Integrated Identity Management System (NIIMS) famously known as Huduma Namba.

Kibicho also denied allegations there was discrimination against any communities during the Huduma Namba registration conducted between April and May 2019.

The PS while appearing in court on Wednesday, October 2, as a witness in a lawsuit filed by Nubian Rights Forum and Kenya Human Rights Commission held that the biometric registration was voluntary and that it was meant to capture crucial national population record.

“We have had cases of people sharing identity card numbers and in many cases, non-Kenyans who have the documents. The system will cure all these. It is also a key decision-making component in providing services, a priority for the government under the Big Four agenda pillars,” he argued.

Kibicho added the system will ensure availability of an accurate and reliable population registration database containing the civil status of the entire population.

"It will also enable verification, validation and authentication of primary data sources, which will ensure adequate planning and enable service delivery," he told a three-judge bench comprised of Justices Pauline Nyamweya, Weldon Korir and Mumbi Ngugi during cross-examination by petitioners' lawyers.

In their petition, the Nubian community and the Kenya Human Rights Commission were challenging constitutionality of the NIIMS.

They wanted the court to compelled the government to guarantee that the system was secure and that personal data collected from Kenyans would be stored in a secure and tamper-proof place, or declare the Huduma Namba registration unconstitutional.
