The latest victim is Thomas Yates of West Bridgford, Nottinghamshire, who found himself collecting used masks and making art out of them, and the end product looked wonderful.

Yates said he was rendered jobless due to COVID-19 and he started using his time clearing the streets by collecting used face masks.

According to a report by the BBC, he has so far collected more than 2,800 abandoned face masks while out running and cycling.

Yates said he always posted his creation on his social media platforms, which has attracted hundreds of followers, adding that he is now addicted to litter picking.

"I have questioned myself. Why am I making art out of litter? And then when you see the end results, I think they're quite good," he said.

A spokesman for Rushcliffe Borough Council applauded him for his noble work and encouraged other people to join him.

"We would like to thank Thomas for his excellent work and all the many volunteers who normally picks litter in their local communities regularly and help keep our streets, parks and open spaces even tidier," he said.
