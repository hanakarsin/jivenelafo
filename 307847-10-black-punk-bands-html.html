10 best black punk bands in 2020

April 16, 2024
Post

Punk rock was first recognized as a music genre in the mid-1970s. The music genre was originally heard in the United States, Australia, and Britain. Punk music was characterized by high pitched vocals, presence of guitars, electric bass, drums, and wild lyrics. Punk artists are known to have a distinct trend when it comes to fashion. Male artists are often in T-Shirts, motorcycle jackets, jeans, and boots. Female artistes rock similar outfits and complement their style with bold and dark makeup. Punk rock initially started as a Caucasian genre. For a while, no black artiste had emerged to sing or form a black punk band. Pure Hell and Death are credited as among the first punk bands which were made of people of colour. Other pioneer black punk bands include X-Ray Spex, Don Letts, Pure Hell, Bad Brains, and Death.

Like other music genres, punk rock has evolved over the years. It was used to describe the garage musicians who made their art in the 60s. The late 70s and early 80s saw punk artistes establish themselves and go mainstream. The growth of the music genre saw other sub-genres emerge.

They include Anarcho Punk, Celtic Punk, Gypsy Punk, Death Punk, Emo, Hardcore, Cowpunk, and Christian. Deathrock is among the sub-genres that have dark lyrics themes of isolation, desolation, and death. The bands continue to grow in the United States, Europe, Asia, Australia and across Africa, breaking the stereotype that punk was meant to be white man's music. This article lists 10 black punk bands that have revolutionized the music scene.

1. Pure Hell

You cannot hold a conversation about punk bands without mentioning Pure Hell. The Philadelphia group is perhaps the first black punk band to have received that much recognition in the 1970s to 1990s. Pure Hell was formed in 1974 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Making music for the group was a bit different because rock as a genre was dominated by white artists.

The original members were Kenny "Stinker" Gordon (vocals, bass on Wild One), Michael "Spider" Sanders (percussions), Preston "Chipper" Morris ( Guitars), and Lenny "Steal" Boles (Bass). The band made their first album 'Noise Addiction' in 1978 but officially released it in 2006.

2. Big Joanie

The all-girl punk band was formed in London in 2013. Big Joanie consists of Stephanie Phillips, Chardine Taylor-Stone and Estella Adeyeri. The band released a debut 'Sistah Punk' in 2014. They dropped another E.P, 'Crooked Room' in 2016. Tepahine Phillips started the group because she felt that there was little intersectionality in the music scene.

She looked for the members online who shared her views on black feminism and Chardine Taylor-Stone, and Kiera Coward-Deyell showed interest. Estelle replaced Kiera in 2017 after the latter moved to Scotland. Kiera was the bassist. Joanie takes pride in being one of the most sought after black female punk rock singers in the UK today.

3. The Muslims

The Muslims officially started performing together in 2017. The band that is made of black and brown people is one of the newest and most loved rock and punk groups in Durham, North Carolina. The group is made up of three members, FaraH BaHbaH (drummer), QADR (vocalist & guitarist), and Abu Shea (bassist).

Their style incorporates sharp lyricism and aggressive satire. The Muslims get inspiration from classic punk and Afropunk roots. The Durham- based band also does rock-rap fusion, hardcore rock, and political satire. They released their recent album, 'Mayo Supreme' on 1st April 2019.

4. The OBGMs

The oOohh Baby Gimme Mores (OBGMs) is a Toronto based black punk band that was formed in 2007. The members are Densil McFarlane (Lead Vocals and Guitar), Colanthony Humphrey (Percussion), Jemuel Roberts (Keyboard, Vocals) and Joseph Brosnan (Bass, Vocals). The OBGMs released their first album, 'The OBGMs' in 2014. The self-titled album talks about life, love, and failure. The group's style is a fusion of hard punk, dirty heavy fuzz and old negroe spiritual. Even with rock as their primary genre, the Canadian band still values Afro-punk.

5. Death

Death was formed in Detroit, Michigan in 1971. The original band members consisted of Bobby Hackney (bass, vocals), David Hackney (guitar), and Dannis Hackney (drums). Bobbie Duncan joined the group later and performed with them in 2009. Bobbie replaced David who died in 2000. The band has undergone a metamorphosis with their name over the years. They first went by 'As RockFire Funk Express' when they played funk, changed to Death when they switched to punk and rock then later called themselves 'The 4th Movement'. They released two rock gospel albums as' the 4th Movement'. The band's debut punk album 'Politicians In My Eyes' was released in 1975 then reissued in 2013.

6. The Brother Moves On

The Brother Moves On (TBMO) is one of the best black rock bands in Africa. The group, which is from Kempton Park, South Africa, was formed around 2008. TBMO had established themselves as graphics and fine artists before moving to rock music. Nkululeko Mthembu and his brother Siyabonga Mthembu first worked as a duo before the other members joined in.

Currently, the band has five members who perform all around South Africa and Africa. The Mthembu brothers came up with the name as they were watching TV series 'The Wire'. The American TV show has a character named The Brother Mouzone. It is from the fictional character that the brothers derived their name. TBMO has four albums and EPs to their name. They released their debut album 'A New Myth' in 2013.

7. Fishbone

The band was formed in 1979 in Los Angeles. The group was labelled as being diverse and distinct. Other than punk rock, they also delved in different genres like funk, soul, reggae, and ska. Original members include Angelo Moore (Saxophone, vocals), John Norwood Fisher (bass), Walter Adam Kibby II (trumpet), Philip "Fish" Fisher (drums), Kendall Jones (guitar), and Christopher Dowd (keyboards, trombone, vocals). Fishbone's rise to fame was due to the subjects they covered in their black punk rock.

They sang about social issues like broken families, racism, nuclear war, fascism, and justice. The band has seven studio albums, having released their first album 'In Your Face' in 1986. They also have 4 Live albums, four compilation albums, and 6 EPs. Currently, the group has 6 active members.

8. Bad Brains

Bad Brains was formed in Washington, D.C. in 1977. It is among the first black punk bands to be created. The group has had a total of 10 members with some members dropping out during the years and new ones joining. Current members are Gary Miller/Dr. Know(lead guitar), Darryl Jenifer (bass) and Paul D. Hudson/H.R. (vocals, occasional rhythm guitar). Bad Brains has released nine studio albums. The Rastafarian group does other genres of music, like metal and reggae.

9. Blk Jks

Blk Jks, also written as BLK JKS originates from Johannesburg South Africa. Two of the band members were childhood friends and worked together even before the others joined. Mpumelelo Mcata and Lindani Buthelezi were childhood friends who grew up in Spruitview, East Rand and formed the group in 2000. Current members include Molefi Makananise (bass) and Tshepang Ramoba (drums). Blk Jks is pronounced as 'Black Jacks'.

The band's breakthrough came in 2008 after record producer, and DJ Diplo noticed them while on tour. The American DJ later brought them to tour the United States. The group also toured Europe in later years and got signed by the label Secretly Canadian. The South African group dropped their first album, 'After Robots', in 2009. They have 3 EPs to their name, BLK JKS 2006, Mystery in 2009 and Zol! In 2010.

10. Unlocking The truth

Based in Brooklyn, New York, Unlocking The Truth consists of Malcolm Brickhouse (guitar, vocals, principal songwriter, composer), Jarad Dawkins (drums), and Alec Atkins (bass). The trio formed the band in 2007. Their first noteworthy show was in Times Square when they were 11. They have since performed in mega concerts and festivals like Coachella Musi & Arts Festival, Heavy Montreal and Lunatic Luau. The band's style can be described as old-school metal blended with contemporary sounds. They are hailed for their speed punk and alternative rock wherever they perform. The Brooklyn band released an album, 'Chaos' in 2016.

Black punk bands have done an excellent job in creating their art and beating cultural stereotypes. Pop culture has also contributed to the growth of rock music as a genre in general. Many of the pioneer bands are still active today, having shows in different parts of the world and making new music for their fans.
