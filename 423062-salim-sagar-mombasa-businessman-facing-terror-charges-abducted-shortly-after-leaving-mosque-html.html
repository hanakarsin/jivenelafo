Salim Sagar: Mombasa Businessman Facing Terror Charges Abducted Shortly after Leaving Mosque

A Mombasa-based businessman who was facing terror charges has reportedly been arrested by unknown people suspected to be officers from the Anti-Terror Police Unit (ATPU).

The Nation on Wednesday, August 18, reported the businessman identified as Abdulhakim Salim Sagar, 40, was fighting terrorism-related charges at a court in Shanzu, Mombasa.

''Since the case started in 2018, he has never skipped any case. I am shocked by the way they arrested my brother in front of his wife and children,” Sagar said.

Salim runs a cyber-business

According to Mbugua Mureithi, the family’s lawyer, Salim, who runs a cyber-business in Old Town, Mombasa, was charged with possessing terror-related materials on his mobile phone.

“We demand those who have taken him to bring him before the court. He has been cooperating with the officers. Even when he was asked to appear at the ATPU offices in Mombasa he oblidged,” Mureithi said.

He indicated that Salim, a father of three, had been reporting to the ATPU offices once every month since 2018.

He was first taken into custody in 2018, following the arrest of his employee.

“In 2018, two men were arrested in the Liboi area, one of whom confirmed to be working for Salim as a gas supplier. Later, the officers arrested him (Mr Salim) and we have been following up on the case since then,” said Mureithi.

Salim was abducted by unknown people while returning home from a nearby mosque at around 6 pm in Kuze, Old Town.

On Thursday, August 12, TUKO.co.ke reported that an elderly man in Maragua, Murang'a county, survived a kidnapping attempt after the car he was ferried in got involved in a road accident.

Thomas Mwaga's family said the old man was walking to a local police post to report a grandson who had refused to go to school before a man and a woman waylaid him.

The incident came two months after Nyeri businessman Gerald Guandaru was kidnapped by a four-person gang on the evening of Tuesday, June 29.

The four-person gang was said to have driven the victim to the Nyeri-Nairobi Highway.

Police officers are still reviewing CCTV footage from the centre as a probe into the incident continues.
