Nitakufinya Duo Matendechere and Musyoka Disclose Their Wives Are Heavily Pregnant

March 09, 2024
Post
  • Matendechere and Musyoka are happy family men
  • The two have been busy multiplying just like God asked man to do after creating him
  • On August 12, the Nitakufinya duo was awarded motorbikes by a local motorcycles dealer company barely a month after landing an ambassadorial role with a flour milling company

Nitakufinya duo has been overworking for the past one month ever since they appeared on Jalang'o TV.

Aaron Matendechere and Musyoka became internet sensations after their Nitakufinya video from 11 years ago went viral a few months ago.

The two were interviewed by Jalang'o and were promised several goodies by different brands and Kenyans also sent them money

Nitakufinya duo talk about their families

Matendechere showed up for the interview with his first wife, but Musyoka was single at that time.

"Niko na bibi wawili na watoto watisa. Bibi was kwanza ndio huyu nimekuja naye (I have two wives and nine children. My first wife is here with me today)," Matendechere said on Bonga na Jalas Show.

During the interview, Musyoka, who does menial jobs to survive, said:

"My biggest wish right now is to have a motorbike so that I stop vending water and go into the bodaboda business."

On August 12, the duo was awarded motorbikes by a local motorcycles dealer company, and during a conversation with Jalang'o, Matendechere revealed that both his wives were pregnant.

Five weeks later, Musyoka is married, and he revealed that his wife is pregnant too.

The two live in Muku Kwa Njenga Slums with their families, and before COVID-19 ravaged the globe, Matendechere used to run a hotel, which he was forced to close down.

What started as a harsh exchange of words between Aron Asiba Matendechere and Musyoka, has turned positive after a flour milling company proposed to have them as brand ambassadors.

Their paths crossed when Musyoka had been approached by a media crew to voice his opinion on the illegal power connections in the slums.

Matendechere, who was passing by, heard Musyoka lament about the menace and interrupted, asking him to ask for employment and shun commenting on matters of electricity.

Jahazi flour will feature them in an advertisement.
