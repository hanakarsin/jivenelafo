Moses Kuria hits out at Aden Duale for supporting parliamentary system

April 24, 2024
Post

- Aden Duale called on the handshake team to recommend for a parliamentary system of government

- He said it will allow all communities to be included in running state affairs

- However, Kuria opined Duale was not genuine with his suggestion and was only seeking to annoy Mt Kenya voters

Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria has hit out at National Assembly Majority Leader Aden Duale of his support for a parliamentary system of government saying the latter is only trying to infuriate Mount Kenya voters.

Duale on Sunday, October 27, took to Facebook to express his opinion in which he held that the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) task force should recommend for parliamentary system to allow inclusion of all ethnic groups in national government.

Duale argued the system would allow free and fair elections to be conducted at constituent level rendering the Supreme Court and Bomas of Kenya irrelevant.

"To my colleagues from Mt Kenya, Kenya belongs to all of us and since independence, Central Kenya has dominated the leadership, and it is high time that we spread both political and economic dominance horizontally along all communities," he said.

However, his sentiments were not taken lightly by Kuria who said the majority leader was not genuine with his suggestion and that he was only positioning himself to benefit from the 2022 presidential elections.

"Don't be fooled that Duale supports a parliamentary system. He knows that by purporting to support a parliamentary system he infuriates Mt Kenya to the core. And that is how Duale and his master will defeat any change by deploying scare crows.Since Duale is already positioning himself to be Comptroller of State House in 2022, there is no way he wants any change in the architecture of Presidency as it is," said Kuria.

He further called out on Duale to face him in challenge for the majority leader position if at all he thinks Mt Kenya votes did not matter.

The sentiments of the two leaders came barely days after the BBI task force disclosed it had prepared a report which is set to be handed over to President Uhuru Kenyatta and Opposition leader Raila Odinga.

Recommendations of the team as far as Constitution amendment is concerned are likely to raise political temperatures ahead of 2022 General Election.
