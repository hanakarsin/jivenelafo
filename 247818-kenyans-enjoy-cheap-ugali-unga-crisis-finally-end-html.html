Kenyans enjoy cheap ugali as Unga crisis finally comes to an end

May 14, 2024
Post

- Many retail outlets in Nairobi are now overflowing with the Sh90 maize flour, with no restriction on the quantity purchased.

- Many supermarkets have since last week stocked all unga brands, effectively ending aggressive scrambling for the commodity.

- The crisis was occasioned by decreased maize production in 2016 as a result of lower volume of rainfall, high costs of farm inputs and the residual effects of Maize Lethal Necrosis Disease.

The unga crisis that rocked the country is seemingly coming to an end, and Kenyans can now enjoy a serving of their favourite meal ,Ugali.

Apparently, many retail outlets in Nairobi and across the country are now overflowing with cheap maize flour, retailing at Sh90 per every 2kgs.

In Nairobi and its environs, many supermarkets have since last week stocked all unga brands and there is no longer restriction on the number of packets one can buy in one go. Aggressive scrambling for unga is now a thing of the past.

“I have for the last two months been regularly passing by Naivas on Thika road to grab some unga, accumulating stock for future use. But this is no longer necessary as they now have a consistent stock on the shelves,” says Patrick Waweru.

His sentiments are echoed by several other shoppers within the Nairobi central business district.

Luckily for shoppers, the maize flour is now cheaper than at any period in recent times after the government launched a subsidy programme on the commodity, officially lowering the price of a two-kilogramme packet from Sh160 to Sh90.

The crisis was occasioned by decreased maize production in 2016 as a result of lower volume of rainfall, high costs of farm inputs and the residual effects of Maize Lethal Necrosis Disease.

According to the Economic Survey 2017, maize production reduced from 42.5 million bags in 2015 to 37.1 million bags in 2016. Many countries across Africa were affected.

As a response to the crisis, the government fast-tracked maize imports into the country, with unaffected countries such as Zambia giving Kenya direct access to maize and sugar and other agricultural products.

Early this month, the government announced that it had identified dozens of posho mills to be allocated imported maize for processing in Nairobi and other regions affected by the maize flour crunch, with the results now showing.

The relief has been boosted by the fact that farmers across the country are enjoying a bumper maize crop harvest following the heavy April rains, reducing over dependence on Unga from the shelves.
